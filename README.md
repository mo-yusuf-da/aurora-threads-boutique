# Aurora Threads Boutique — Sales Dashboard

**Tool:** Power BI &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Domain:** Fashion Retail &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Period:** May 2025

---

## Project Overview

This project delivers an interactive **Power BI sales dashboard** for **Aurora Threads Boutique**, a high-end fashion retailer specialising in designer clothing, footwear, and accessories across three store locations. The boutique was struggling to derive actionable insights from its growing sales data — facing challenges in identifying product trends, tracking cross-store performance, and making timely, data-driven decisions.

The dashboard was built to give the business a single, dynamic view of sales performance, customer behaviour, and product trends — filterable by store location in real time.

This project was completed as part of the **10Alytics Data Analytics programme** and demonstrates end-to-end dashboard design in Power BI.

## Dataset

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Date | Date of the sales transaction |
| Store | Store location (Store A, Store B, Store C) |
| Product | Product name or description |
| Category | Product category (Clothing, Footwear, Accessories) |
| Gender | Target market for the product (Male, Female, Unisex) |
| Quantity | Number of units sold |
| Sales Amount | Revenue for each transaction |
| Customer ID | Unique identifier for each customer |
| Discount | Discount applied to the transaction (if any) |
| Payment Type | Payment method (Cash, Credit Card, Debit Card, Online Payment) |

## Key Findings

- **$288K** total revenue from **771 transactions** across 3 store locations and **31 customers**
- **Accessories is the top-performing category** at $148K (51.3% of total revenue), more than double Clothing ($71K) and Footwear ($70K) combined
- **Handbag is the single best-selling product** at $24K, followed by Necklace ($22K) and Sunglasses ($19K) — all Accessories
- **Unisex products generate the most revenue at $121K (41.9%)**, ahead of Female ($102K / 35.4%) and Male ($65K / 22.7%), suggesting the boutique's cross-gender lines are its strongest commercial performers
- **Cash is the most used payment method at $88K (30.6%)**, followed closely by Online Payment ($87K / 30.1%) — together accounting for over 60% of all transactions
- **Sales trend shows a volatile weekly pattern** with peaks reaching $24K and troughs dropping to $2K within the same month, suggesting demand is highly event- or promotion-driven
- The **store-level slicer** (Store A / B / C) allows performance to be isolated per location, enabling management to identify which stores are driving or lagging revenue

## Dashboard Features

- **KPI Cards** — Total Revenue, Total Quantity Sold, Number of Stores, Total Customers
- **Bar Charts** — Revenue by Product Category, Top 10 Products by Revenue
- **Pie Charts** — Revenue by Gender, Revenue by Payment Type
- **Line Chart** — Total Revenue trend over time (daily)
- **Customer Table** — Individual customer revenue and quantity purchased
- **Store Slicer** — Filter all visuals by Store A, B, or C simultaneously
